Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is clean energy? - Clean energy refers to energy sources that produce minimal or no pollution during their generation, promoting environmental sustainability.

Q: How can I contribute to promoting affordable energy? - You can contribute by supporting initiatives, spreading awareness, and adopting energy-efficient practices in your daily life.

Q: Are there government initiatives for clean energy? - Yes, many governments worldwide have initiated programs and policies to promote the development and use of clean and renewable energy sources.

Q: What are the benefits of affordable energy? - Affordable energy promotes economic development, reduces poverty, and ensures access to essential services for all.

Q: How can renewable energy contribute to affordable energy solutions? - Renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind, provide sustainable alternatives, reducing long-term costs and promoting affordability.

Q: What steps can individuals take to lower their energy bills and contribute to affordability? - Adopting energy-efficient practices, using energy-saving appliances, and exploring renewable options at home can help reduce personal energy expenses.

Q: How do NGOs contribute to promoting affordable energy solutions? - NGOs play a vital role by advocating for policy changes, facilitating community engagement, and implementing projects that increase access to affordable and sustainable energy sources.

Q: What specific initiatives do NGOs undertake to address energy affordability challenges? - NGOs often implement awareness campaigns, support local communities with energy-efficient technologies, and collaborate with stakeholders to develop and implement policies that enhance energy affordability.

Affordable and Clean Energy: A choice or a must for Africa?

The UN sustainable Development Goal 7 (SDG) “Affordable and Clean Energy” is expected to be achieved by 2030. This goal aims to i) ensure universal access to affordable, reliable and modern energy services, ii) substantially increase the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix, and iii) double the global rate of improvement in energy efficiency. In addition to its direct impact on improving livelihoods, access to modern, sustainable energy services is often regarded as a necessity for achieving all of the other SDGs.
